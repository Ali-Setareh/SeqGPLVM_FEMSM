\section{Fixed effects model}
The fixed-effect marginal structuran model (FE-MSM) proposed 
by \cite{blackwellEffectPoliticalAdvertising2024} 
can be seen as a very direct way of bringing back the sequential 
ignorability assumption where we suspect of unmeasured time-invariant confounding. 
instead of trying to model the unmeasured confounder explicitly, 
we include a unit-specific fixed effect in the propensity score 
model that is used to construct IPTW weights. More formally, the identification of 
the causal effect of interest relies on the following assumption:
\begin{assumpFE}[Unit-specific ignorability]\label{ass:FE-unit-specific-ignor}
    \[
    \{ Y_{i,t+1}(\bar{a}_{t}),\, \bar X_{i,t+1}(\bar a_t) \}
\;\perp\!\!\!\perp\;
A_{it}
\;\big|\;
\bar X_{it},\,
\bar A_{i,t-1} = \bar a_{t-1},\,
Z_i. \]
\end{assumpFE}
