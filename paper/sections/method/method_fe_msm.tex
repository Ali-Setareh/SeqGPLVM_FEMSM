\subsection{Fixed effects model}
\label{sec:fe-msm}
The fixed-effect marginal structuran model (FE-MSM) proposed 
by \cite{blackwellEffectPoliticalAdvertising2024} 
can be seen as a very direct way of bringing back the sequential 
ignorability assumption where we suspect of unmeasured time-invariant confounding. 
Instead of trying to model the unmeasured confounder explicitly, 
we include a unit-specific fixed effect in the propensity score 
model that is used to construct IPTW weights. More formally, the identification of 
the causal effect of interest relies on the following assumption:
\begin{idassumpFE}[Unit-specific ignorability]\label{ass:FE-unit-specific-ignor}
    Let $Z_i$ be a time-invariant unmeasured random variable, For 
    all $i$, $t$ and $\bar{a}_t$,
    \[
    \{ Y_{i,t+1}(\bar{a}_{t}),\, \bar X_{i,t+1}(\bar a_t) \}
\;\perp\!\!\!\perp\;
A_{it}
\;\big|\;
\bar X_{it},\,
\bar A_{i,t-1} = \bar a_{t-1},\,
Z_i. \]
\end{idassumpFE}

Assumption \ref{ass:FE-unit-specific-ignor} states that conditioned on 
the past treatment and covariate history, as well as 
the unmeasured time-invariant confounder substitute $Z_i$, 
the treatment assignment at time $t$ is independent of 
the potential outcomes process and covariates process at time $t+1$. 

\begin{modassumpFE}[Correct specification of the propensity score model up to time-invariant unmeasured heterogeneity]
\label{ass:FE-ps-correct}
There exists an \emph{unmeasured}, unit-specific, time-constant variable $Z_i$ capturing
time-invariant unmeasured heterogeneity in treatment assignment, and a known model class
$\{\pi_{it}(\cdot;\theta,z)\}_{\theta\in\Theta}$ (parametric in $\theta$), such that for all individuals $i$
and times $t$,
\[
\Pr\!\bigl(D_{it}=1 \,\big|\, \bar X_{it},\, \bar A_{i,t-1},\, Z_i \bigr)
=
\pi_{it}\!\left(\bar X_{it},\, \bar A_{i,t-1};\, \theta_0,\, Z_i\right),
\]
for some finite-dimensional parameter vector $\theta_0$. In other words, the functional form
in $\theta$ is correctly specified \emph{conditional on} $Z_i$ (i.e., the model is correct \emph{up to}
the unmeasured heterogeneity $Z_i$).
\end{modassumpFE}
